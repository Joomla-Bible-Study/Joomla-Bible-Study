 3.4.2 Oral Hygiene & Dental Health Tips for Children

Dental hygiene is important for young children. As children get their baby, and permanent teeth, the dental process should be a top consideration. Pediatric Dentist, Dr. Sacha Cargill, offers the following recommendations to ensure a healthy dental future for our little ones.

 

  • Establish a dental relationship early. Schedule a dental visit for your child within six months of the first tooth eruption, or by your child’s first birthday
  • Limit bottles at bedtime to ONLY water. Napping or sleeping with juice or milk can cause childhood decay due to sugars from the liquids staying in direct contact with the teeth for prolonged periods.
  • Clean pacifiers often, and properly.
  • Clean gums while teeth are erupting. Baby teeth are at risk for decay as soon as they appear.
  • Prevent bad habits. Thumb sucking and pacifiers after age two can create crowded and crooked teeth, or bite problems.
  • Play it safe. If your child plays any sports, make sure they wear a mouth guard. Mouth guards can help prevent injury to the jaw, mouth, and teeth. A mouth guard is significantly less expensive that the costs of repairs due to injury.
  • Select healthier treats. Avoid giving children treats that are sour, (high in acidity, which erodes tooth enamel. Avoid treats that are sticky or chewy, (leaves sugars directly on the teeth for prolonged periods). Some healthier snack options may include cereal bars, pretzels, or granola bars.

The Event Liaison Coordinator will be a facilities key-holder and able to open the church facilities as needed for rehearsals, weddings, or other planned events.

 

  • The Event Liaison Coordinator will schedule and confirm all AV and Media personnel that is needed for the event to run smoothly.
  • The Event Liaison Coordinator will receive training on how to set the temperature controls for the facilities as needed.
  • The Event Liaison Coordinator will approve all movement of furniture to and from the stage, foyers, hallways, and rooms in the facilities.
